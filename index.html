Wine
on the
Edge

Nisyros is a Volcano. Alive. It breathes, it trembles, it reminds you who is in charge. We make our wine here — on the edge of the crater.

1 / 7
Almost
Nothing

Minimal intervention in winemaking is a form of love and respect. Our job is to protect without interfering. We step back so the vine can speak.

2 / 7
With the
Land

The volcanic soil of Nisyros is a blessed land. We are guests and, for a moment, caretakers. For millennia agriculture sustained life on the island. We work to revive the fundamental values of that tradition, with respect and endless dedication. The vine must live here long after we are gone.

3 / 7
On the
Shoulders
of Giants

We read the books, sat with our elders, learned the local traditions. We study fermentation, microbiology, viticulture — everything that happens inside every tank and bottle. We respect the rules of physics and nature: temperature, pressure, time, climate, seasons. Knowledge is our freedom.

4 / 7
We try
things that
Fail

We look for rare vineyards, ferment in unusual vessels, blend the unexpected. Failure is an essential part of creation. But sometimes something extraordinary happens. And nothing leaves our cellar unless we are really proud of it.

5 / 7
We Do
What we Say

We share the story, the vineyard, the vintage, every step of the process. The bright moments, the dark hours. We do what we say. We say what we do. That's what you will find in the bottle.

6 / 7
A Wine
that
Belongs

Every bottle carries the energy of the Aegean sea, a place unlike any other. The sun that burns without apology. The sea that holds the light at sunset. The wind and the waves. The fire beneath your feet. These islands are vibrant — and so is the wine they give us. Unique, unrepeatable. True to the land. Clean in the glass.
