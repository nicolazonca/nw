Athiri

Story

The moment before it begins. Dry-farmed Athiri from old Dodecanesian terraces — hand-tended, extremely low yields, harvested when the decision can no longer be delayed.

Winemaking & Tasting Notes

Minimal intervention. Whole-cluster pressed, temperature-controlled ferment in stainless steel. Nothing added, nothing removed. Bone dry, precise, saline. Citrus pith, green almond, chalky minerality.

40 Milia [konda]

Athiri · Mandilaria

Story

40 nautical miles — the distance from Nisyros to the nearest mainland coast. That is how far you are from anything ordinary when you drink this wine.

Winemaking & Tasting Notes

Mavrothiriko and Assyrtiko from old dry-farmed terraces. Extended maceration, gentle extraction, aged in used oak. Iron, dark cherry, volcanic dust. Firm tannins and a long, mineral-driven finish.

Mandilaria · Athiri · Rosé

Story

In the Dodecanese, the pomegranate is called 'roudia.' Like its fruit, this wine reveals a deep, jewelled red.

Mandilaria and Athiri, co-fermented, harvested early for pure fruit energy. Semi-carbonic, indigenous yeasts, zero compromise. Drink chilled. Here's to endless summer sunsets.

Athiri · Amphora White

Story

The warm air that breathes through the cracks of the volcano is called Apyri. This wine takes its name from that breath.

Athiri, part skin-contact, rested in amphora. Unclarified, unfiltered. Wild ferment, no additions. Golden amber with copper glints. Beeswax, dried herbs, white peach. Textured palate with saline finish.
