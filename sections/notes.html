Cellar·15 March 2026

The Last Pressing of '25

Three barrels left from the cool side of the vineyard. We waited as long as the season allowed, and then the wind told us it was time.

The grapes had been hanging since late September, longer than any of our previous vintages. We had decided early in the summer that this lot would be our patience experiment — we wanted to see what extra weeks on the vine would teach the fruit, and what it would teach us.

By the morning of the pressing the cellar smelled different. Not of the green ferment of harvest, but of something quieter, more concentrated. A small crowd of friends came to help. We pressed by hand, slowly, and stopped when the juice ran cloudy. There is something in this wine we have never tasted before. We will know more in two years.
